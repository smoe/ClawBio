Part 2

Variant Interpretation

The biology before the AI

Genomic variation

4-5M

SNPs per person

  • SNP: Single Nucleotide Polymorphism. One DNA letter changed (e.g., A → G)
  • Indel: Insertion or deletion of a few bases
  • CNV: Copy Number Variant. Large segments duplicated or deleted

Most variants are benign. A small fraction affect protein function, drug metabolism, or disease risk. The challenge: finding the ones that matter.

ACMG five-tier classification

CategoryMeaningClinical action
PathogenicCauses or contributes to diseaseReport. Genetic counselling.
Likely pathogenicStrong evidence, not conclusiveReport with caveat.
VUSUncertain significanceDo not act on. May be reclassified.
Likely benignProbably no clinical effectGenerally not reported.
BenignNo disease associationNot reported.

Over half of all known variants are VUS. "We don't know yet" is often the most honest answer in genomics.

VCF

Variant Call Format. The standard file for storing genomic variants.

VEP

Ensembl Variant Effect Predictor. Determines functional consequence (missense, frameshift, etc.).

ClinVar

NCBI database of variant-disease associations. Is this variant known to be harmful?

gnomAD

Population allele frequencies across 76,000+ genomes. How common is this variant?

Pharmacogenomics

Your genes determine how you metabolise drugs. The wrong dose can be ineffective or dangerous.

GeneDrugs (examples)If you are a poor metaboliser

The warfarin example

Warfarin is a blood thinner with a very narrow safe zone. Two genes determine the right dose:

CYP2C9

Breaks down warfarin.

Variants *2 and *3 slow metabolism. Drug stays active longer. Lower dose needed.

VKORC1

The drug's target.

TT genotype = higher sensitivity. Less drug needed for the same effect. Lower dose needed.

The Corpasome carries both. Standard warfarin dose would risk serious haemorrhage. This is the textbook example of pharmacogenomics saving lives.

Part 3

Equity in Genomics

The bias in genomic data

86%of GWAS participants are European
30xmore BRCA data for Europeans
80%PRS accuracy loss in non-Europeans

A variant that looks "benign" in current databases may be pathogenic in an unstudied population. AI trained on biased data amplifies the problem.

VUS in one population may be pathogenic in another. Every analysis should consider which populations the reference databases represent.
